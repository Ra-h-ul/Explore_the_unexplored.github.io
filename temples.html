Bhavani temple at tuljapur

Kailash temple at ellora

It may not be officially declared as one of the best wonders of the world, but no one can deny the greatness of the Kailash Temple in Ellora. Situated about 30 km from the city of Aurangabad in Maharashtra, the rock-cave temple of Ellora is the largest monolithic structure in the world. It is believed that the Kailash Temple at Ellora has striking similarities to the Virupaksha temple of northern Karnataka.

Kopeshwar temple

Kopeshwar Temple is at Khidrapur, Kolhapur district, Maharashtra. It is at the border of Maharashtra and Karnataka. It is also accessible from Sangli as well. It was built in the 12th century by Shilahara king Gandaraditya between 1109 and 1178 CE. It is to the east of Kolhapur, ancient & artistic on the bank of the Krishna river. Panchganga temple

This temple is present in the same premises as Mahabaleshwar Temple. This is referred to as Panchganga Temple as it is located at the converge of five rivers - Krishna, Venna, Savitri, Koyna and Gayatri. The water from the five rivers converge here and it comes out of a pipe and falls into the tank beneath. The water is sacred. Devotees were queued up to touch the water, put it on their head and drink it. The temple was constructed in 13th Century. Maintenance can be better, mainly with reference to cleanliness.

Parvati temple

Parvati Hill is a hillock in Pune, India. The hillock rises to 2,100 feet (640 m) above sea level. Atop the hillock is the Parvati Temple, one of the most scenic locations in Pune. The temple is the oldest heritage structure in Pune and was built during the rule of the Peshwa dynasty.[1] For visitors, Parvati hill is also an observation point that offers a panoramic view of Pune. It is the second highest point in Pune (after Vetal Hill)
