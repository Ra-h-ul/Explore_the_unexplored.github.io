Bhimashankar

Bhimashankar is one of the holiest hill stations in Maharashtra, nay the country. The hill station is known for the religious story and presence of a number of temples in the vicinity. This place also houses a Jyotirlinga that just adds to the grace of this place. But these are not the only places worth visiting in Bhimashankar. The Bhimashankar Wildlife sanctuary is one of the most notable places to visit in Bhimashankar.

Chikhaldara

Located in Amravati, Chikhaldara offers majestic views and excellent wildlife. Chikhaldara is the only place in the region that produces coffee making it a coffee lovers destination. The rich smell of the coffee engulfs the place and will put you in a trance as soon as you set foot in the area. The magnificent view of the hills coupled with the glory of the Semadoh lake is the highlight of this place.

Durshet

Durshet, now one of the most beautiful hill stations of Maharashtra, in the 1600s was a battlefield for Shivaji Maharaj. The hill station is an archaeological marvel. The main highlights of this place are the two Ganesh temples and Uddhar hot spring. This place is the perfect destination for people looking to spend some time in the company of nature.

Igatpuri

Close to Mumbai and Pune, it could be the famous gateway spot. Igatpuri has the largest Vipassana meditation academy and offers immense natural beauty and solitude to your mind and soul. The pleasant climate, breath-taking valleys, sparkling waterfalls, and vintage forts makes Igatpuri a must visit destination.

Jawhar

Jawhar is one of the most offbeat hill stations in Maharashtra, located in Thane region. Surrounded by cascading waterfalls and luxuriant hill, Jawhar features natural splendour like Hanuman Point, Jaisagar dam, and Dabosa waterfall as well as some architectural marvels like Jai Villas Palace and Shipramal. Among the perfect monsoon getaways near Pune and Mumbai, Jawhar also has ages old and mesmerizing tribal art known as Warli paintings.

Khandala

Located very close to Lonavala is Khandala, it has lush green forests, misty mountains, sparkling waterfalls and wonderful views. The picturesque Khandala is one of the cleanest and romantic hill stations near Kolhapur. With majestic hill forts and hill caves, enchanting viewpoints, and trekking paths, it is a perfect abode for adventure enthusiasts as well as lovebirds.

Koroli

Nestled in nature’s lap, Koroli is another not so popular and not so crowded hill stations in Maharashtra. Once you are here, enjoy the beauty of gorgeous valleys, lush green fields, amazing weather and enchanting quietude. One of the best hill stations in Maharashtra for an offbeat vacation, Koroli is a heaven for hikers, trekkers, nature lovers, and photography enthusiasts. This place is also dotted by some of the most astounding villas in Maharashtra.

Karjat

Being famous for farmhouses, Karjat is the last stop of Thane district and is also one of the remote villages that is yet to be explored by many travelers. Though this place is slightly popular amongst trekkers, this place doesn’t have many visitors which adds to the peace that this village has to offer and is perfect for a reviving escapade from your daily routine.

Lavasa

Lavasa is a hill station in Maharashtra that is relatively unexplored. Thanks to the magnificent backdrops, this place is getting famous amongst couples for their pre-wedding shoots. Lavasa is a newly developed hill station that is slowly and steadily gaining recognition as one of the popular hill stations. The hill station combines natural beauty with modern entertainment options. Visit this place if you are looking to spend some amazing time in the lap of nature coupled with entertainment from the city.

Malshej Ghat

Idyllic for adventure buffs and nature enthusiasts. Situated 85 kms from Kalyan, Malshej Ghat has won the hearts of all kinds of travellers with its natural marvels and architectural wonders. From numerous waterfalls and spectacular dams to hill forts and some of the most famous age old temples in Maharashtra, it has everything one can ask for.

While there are less number of attractions here such as the Rajmachi fort, you can always land in this place for a weekend getaway from your bustling city life. Sawantwadi

Tucked between Sahyadri hills on one side and coastal road on the other, Sawantwadi has peaceful environs, serene lakes, vintage temples, plush green forests, and sumptuous authentic Konkani food. Except exploring Sawantwadi Palace and Moti Talao Lake, the place has nothing much to do. Sawantwadi is basically for those in search for a tranquil place filled with natural wonders.

Wai

Easily accessible from Mumbai and Pune. If you want to discover mesmerizing hill stations of Maharashtra, then you must go for Wai. It has Buddhist caves; one can find serenity here. The Buddhist caves becomes the fundamental reason of visiting, which is an eye-catching place. The tourists will get to see amazing views of the mountains which is indeed mesmerizing. There are numerous places in Wai which you must visit when in Wai such as dams, caves, temples, lakes and forts.
